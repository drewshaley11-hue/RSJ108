How to be an Investigative Journalist

Assembly time: 15 minutes
Effectiveness rating: 100% effective

Are you wondering how to be an investigative journalist? Follow these steps to find out!

  1. Write about the political, but don’t act political

    Focus on facts, not opinions—opinions belong in an op-ed column. Bleach your personal and political opinions out of your stories.

  2. Report from the beginning

    Investigative journalism takes time. Keep your reporting incremental and update your editor regularly.

  3. Documentation is key

    Organize, save, and make copies of everything. Documentation is essential in the age of misinformation.

  4. Find sources who are witnesses and participants

    Start at the bottom of the chain of command and work your way up.

  5. Always protect your sources

    Meet where they’re comfortable. Use secure communication methods and never reveal your source.

  6. Start interviews by establishing ground rules

    Be transparent about recording and whether the conversation is on or off the record.

  7. Be upfront and civil during your interview

    Ask relevant questions in order. Stay calm and verify everything.

  8. Pivot your story if something new develops

    Be flexible. Stories evolve and sometimes change direction entirely.

  9. Fact-check everything

    Names, dates, addresses, quotes — verify it all.
